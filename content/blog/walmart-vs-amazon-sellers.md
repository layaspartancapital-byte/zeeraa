# Walmart vs Amazon: Which Marketplace Is Better for Sellers?

The Amazon vs Walmart debate is one of the biggest decisions eCommerce sellers face. Both platforms offer massive reach, but they differ significantly in fees, competition, and growth dynamics.

## Traffic and Reach

Amazon dominates with over 300 million active customer accounts and 2+ billion monthly visits. Walmart.com is growing fast with 120+ million monthly visitors and the backing of 4,700+ physical stores.

**Winner:** Amazon for raw traffic. Walmart for growth trajectory.

## Fees and Costs

Amazon charges referral fees (8-15%), FBA fees, and a $39.99/month Professional seller fee. Walmart charges referral fees (6-15%) with no monthly subscription and no storage fees for seller-fulfilled items.

**Winner:** Walmart for lower overall fees, especially for FBM sellers.

## Competition

Amazon has over 2 million active sellers. Walmart Marketplace has approximately 150,000 sellers. Less competition means more visibility per product on Walmart.

**Winner:** Walmart for less competition and easier organic visibility.

## Fulfillment

Amazon's FBA network is unmatched — 2-day Prime shipping is the standard. Walmart's WFS (Walmart Fulfillment Services) is catching up with competitive rates and 2-day shipping badges.

**Winner:** Amazon for fulfillment infrastructure. Walmart for fulfillment cost.

## Advertising

Amazon Advertising is mature with Sponsored Products, Brands, Display, and DSP. Walmart Connect is growing with lower CPCs and less advertiser competition.

**Winner:** Amazon for sophistication. Walmart for ROI potential.

## The Verdict

The answer isn't either/or — it's both. The most successful eCommerce brands sell on multiple platforms. Start where your products fit best, then expand. Zeeraa manages both platforms simultaneously, tailoring strategy for each marketplace's unique dynamics.
