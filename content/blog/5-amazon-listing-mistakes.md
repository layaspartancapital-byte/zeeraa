# 5 Amazon Listing Mistakes That Kill Your Sales

Selling on Amazon is competitive, and your product listing is your only salesperson. Yet most sellers make critical mistakes that silently kill their conversion rates and rankings.

## 1. Generic, Keyword-Stuffed Titles

Your title needs to balance keywords with readability. Amazon's algorithm rewards relevance, but shoppers reward clarity. The best titles include your brand name, key product feature, size/quantity, and primary keyword — without reading like a spam message.

**Fix:** Write your title for humans first, then optimize for keywords. Front-load the most important information.

## 2. Weak Bullet Points

Bullet points are where you sell. Too many sellers list features without explaining benefits. "Made of stainless steel" means nothing — "Made of surgical-grade stainless steel that won't rust, chip, or stain — guaranteed for life" sells.

**Fix:** Use the Feature → Benefit → Proof framework for every bullet point.

## 3. Low-Quality Images

Amazon gives you 7+ image slots, and most sellers use 3-4 mediocre photos. Your main image drives clicks, and your gallery drives conversions. Lifestyle images, infographics, and comparison charts all matter.

**Fix:** Use all available image slots. Include lifestyle photos, size references, feature callouts, and social proof.

## 4. Ignoring Backend Search Terms

Backend search terms are hidden keywords that help Amazon understand your product. Most sellers either leave them empty or stuff them with irrelevant terms.

**Fix:** Fill all 250 bytes with relevant search terms you haven't used in your title or bullets. Include common misspellings and Spanish translations.

## 5. No A+ Content

If you're brand registered and not using A+ Content, you're leaving money on the table. A+ Content can increase conversions by 5-10% and provides additional keyword indexing.

**Fix:** Create A+ Content with comparison charts, brand story modules, and detailed feature breakdowns.
